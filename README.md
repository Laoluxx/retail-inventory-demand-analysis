# Retail Inventory & Demand Analysis

## Business Problem

A retail company wants to understand product demand and inventory risk in order to reduce stockouts, avoid overstocking, and improve overall sales performance.

---

## Objective

To analyze sales data and simulate inventory levels in order to identify high-demand categories, slow-moving categories, and areas with potential stock risk.

---

## Tools Used

* Python (pandas, numpy, matplotlib)
* Jupyter Notebook

---

## Key Analysis Performed

* Data cleaning and preprocessing
* Sales performance analysis by product category
* Identification of slow-moving categories
* Inventory simulation using demand-based modeling
* Stock risk analysis (Quantity vs Inventory)
* Time-based demand trend analysis

---

## Key Insights

* Clothing has the highest stock risk at **41.9%**, indicating a strong likelihood of stockouts if demand continues at the current rate
* Electronics follows closely with a stock risk of **40.1%**, showing similar inventory pressure
* Beauty has the lowest stock risk at **39.9%**, but still operates at a relatively high demand-to-stock ratio
* All product categories are operating at approximately **40% stock risk**, suggesting that inventory levels may be tight relative to demand

---

## Business Recommendations

* Prioritize restocking for Clothing and Electronics categories to prevent potential stockouts
* Continuously monitor inventory levels across all categories due to overall high stock risk
* Improve inventory planning by aligning stock levels more closely with demand patterns
* Use demand trends to anticipate high-demand periods and stock accordingly

---

---

## Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates how combining sales data with simulated inventory can provide actionable insights into stock management. By identifying high-risk categories and demand trends, businesses can make better decisions to optimize inventory, reduce lost sales, and improve operational efficiency.
